- Look at the number of swings. The more number of swings on the left, similarly more will be on right.
- After hitting a large time frame objective, Market will balance itself by going beyond the leg that BOS, and balance the leg above it, or dump orders above it.
    
    ![](BackTest%20Journal/image.png)
    
- This is the reason why some structures have "W" type market structure as shown in the pic. Before hitting an important area, market forms an OB , thus when balancing them, it forms such structure and market does not goes below 2 as there will be market structure shift otherwise.
    
    First market hits one important level in 1, then completes the move in 2, and then finishes mitigation in 3.
    
    ![](BackTest%20Journal/image%201.png)
    
- Such candles with major movement like this huge hammer is, they act as a good OB. There are two supposed OB here, first is the huge hammer and second is the continuous Green candles just right to the hammer. On the right, market just touches the hammer OB and reacts the way it should.

- Market is divided into sections when the market is showing it’s going to go in both direction. Divide the market based on the direction and structure and set limits on major swing levels of each section. Market will eventually follow the higher time-frame but it’s not going to do that unless the lower time frame opposite market direction is fulfilled.

- Market is supposed to respect the major swing levels in a trend. There are also minor levels in a swing leg of a major leg, whose violation can be crucial in determining the market.
    
    ![image.png](BackTest%20Journal/image%206.png)
    
    In this, major levels are shown by red and minor levels in a major leg is shown by white. Major levels are strictly taken on the basis of body of the candles and not the wicks. We need a good break above a level and not just grazing it to be called a major swing break. 
    
    See that when in the retracement, market broke a minor swing low in a major swing, it again broke below the low giving the boost for the up trend and not violating the previous trend.
    
- In the  market, after you’ve created a range, when market crosses the 50% level then, if the bias is bearish, it’s going to take all the liquidity above the 50% level to dump below. That is not change in delivery in upward side but liquidity gathering. It’ll continue to do so until there’s a BOS in downward direction. Once that happens market will not seek the upward liquidity and continue it’s path i.e. downward.

- Market works as a wave, it goes up, retraces and then goes up again (in bullish case). This retracement can be governed by looking at the BOS area of the market.

Below is an illustrated picture of the market that shows the area where market BOS above, that was the first BOS i.e. that was the major swing high , which market BOS up before again going above and taking out another swing high. 

Then you can see that market began a small bearish trend which ended just around the level of the BOS 4H mark and eventually went up.
    
    ![image.png](BackTest%20Journal/image%2011.png)
    
    At that area, you can see that again market makes a BOS, that breaks the major swing high and also the low, making that pattern where BOS happens on both direction. 
    
    So the hypothesis is that look for the BOS level if you are looking the extent of the retracement. When retracing market will get into a temporary opposite trend. That trend will stop at that level only. And when it reaches that level, there is a high probability that market shows a stop hunt at that level. So be aware
